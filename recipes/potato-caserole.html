Potato Casserole

The potato casserole is great for vegetarians looking to get their healthy or unhealthy dose of cheese!

The potato casserole was first created in 1950 after the Second World War. Its creators had hoped to make a dish so irresistible that anyone who tried it would immediately give up any ambitions or hopes to conquer the world or do bad to others. In other words, the potato casserole became the first technology ever created with the sole intention of bringing about world peace.

As the potato casserole began making its way around the world, people were shocked, not only by its immaculate taste, but also for its incredibly beneficial psychological effects. Word quickly spread about its incredbly properties and soon everybody wanted to try a bite of this exquisite dish. As the potato casserole and its followers began to grow around the world, so did its enemies and the people who wanted to see its destruction. Eventually, two new superpowers arose with one side supporting the potato casserole and one seeking to destroy it. As nuclear war seemed to be the inevitable end, both sides struck a truce which allowed the potato casserole to retain its tasty properties but would have to forgo its tranquil psychological effects.

Ingredients

  • 1 pint (two cups) sour cream
  • 1 can (10 3/4 oz) condensed cream of chicken soup
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted
  • 1 tablespoon garlic salt
  • 1 cup chopped onions
  • 1 bag (30 oz) frozen country-style shredded hash brown potatoes, partially thawed
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese (8 oz)

Steps

  1. Heat oven to 350°F.
  2. In large bowl, combine 1 pint (2 cups) sour cream, 1 can (10 3/4 oz) condensed cream of chicken soup, 1/2 cup melted butter, 1 tablespoon garlic salt, 1 cup chopped onions, 1 bag (30 oz) frozen country-style shredded hash brown potatoes, partially thawed, and 2 cups shredded Cheddar cheese (8 oz); stir until well blended.
  3. Pour into ungreased 13x9-inch (3-quart) glass baking dish.
  4. Bake 45 minutes.
